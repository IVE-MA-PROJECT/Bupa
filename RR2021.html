In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, Bupa proudly and actively supported our customers in various ways including:

Good Private Health Insurance does come at a cost
 
We appreciate that any increase in insurance premiums can be tough, especially right now. But we want to help ensure we can continue to deliver quality services to our 4 million customers in Australia. Increases in premiums are related to the rising costs of providing health care – partly driven by things like our ageing population, medical advances, inflation and an increase in people using hospital services.

What's changing in your cover?
 
  Removal of Outpatient pregnancy coverage: From 1 June 2021, Outpatient pregnancy coverage will be removed from *|PKG_DESC|*. Outpatient pregnancy means any medical consultations or treatment associated with pregnancy without the need to be admitted into the hospital. For example: Obstetrician, Midwife, Antenatal Consultations and any Radiology (Ultrasounds) and Pathology services. If you're currently pregnant and your due date is on or before 1 December 2021, you'll continue to be covered for outpatient pregnancy services until this date. If coverage for these services is important to you, you can upgrade to another cover that includes outpatient pregnancy by contacting us to discuss your options.
 
  Introduction of 2-month wait period for Palliative care, Rehabilitation and Hospital psychiatric services: From 1 June 2021, we'll be introducing a 2-month waiting period across all services for Palliative care, Rehabilitation and Hospital psychiatric services on *|PKG_DESC|*. At the moment, the waiting period only applies for pre-existing conditions. The waiting period starts on the date you enter Australia, or when you start your membership (whichever is the later date). Once the waiting period ends, you'll receive all the benefits for these services detailed in your cover.
 
  Removal of Rehabilitation coverage: From 1 June 2021, Rehabilitation will be removed from *|PKG_DESC|*. Rehabilitation refers to inpatient and admitted day patient rehabilitation related to surgery or illness (such as inpatient and admitted day patient rehabilitation for stroke recovery). If you (or anyone on this cover) is admitted into hospital for Rehabilitation prior to 1 June 2021, we'll continue to cover you under your current policy until 1 September 2021.
 
  Adding $250 Excess: From 1 June 2021, we'll be adding a $250 excess to *|PKG_DESC|*. An excess is a one-off payment (per person/per year) you make on admission to Hospital for a same day or overnight stay. If you (or anyone on this policy) have pre-booked a Hospital admission before 22 February 2021, and the procedure will take place between 1 June 2021 until 1 July 2021, the $250 excess won't apply. The excess will not apply to child dependants on your policy.
 
  Removal of Bone Marrow Transfusion or Transplant and Organ Transplant services: From 1 June 2021, we'll be removing cover for Bone Marrow transfusion or transplant and organ transplant services from *|PKG_DESC|*. If you have pre-booked your Hospital admission before 22 February 2021, and the procedure will take place between 1 June 2021 until 1 July 2021 you'll be covered under your current policy for Bone Marrow transfusion or transplant and Organ Transplant procedures.
 
  Removal of Non-Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) listed pharmacy from outpatient pharmacy services: From 1 June 2021, benefits will no longer be paid for non-PBS listed pharmacy from outpatient pharmacy services on Short Stay Visitors Cover. You'll still receive benefits of up to $50 per script item (up to a maximum of $300 per person per calendar year) on selected PBS listed pharmacy items. This may include things like medication prescribed to you when leaving hospital. If this service is important to you please contact us to discuss your options.

What are we adding to your cover?
 
We're pleased to let you know that the following service is being added to your cover:
 
  Introduction of Outpatient Allied Health Services: From 1 April 2021, if you've been referred by your General Practitioner (GP) to an Allied Health professional, and have a Health Management Plan in place to treat your condition, you may be eligible to claim some or all of the cost of Allied Health services that you use. Allied Health providers are a broad range of health professionals such as dietitians, physiotherapists, podiatrists, speech pathologists and psychologists. Keep in mind that Doctors, nurses and dentists (and other oral health professionals) are not Allied Health professionals.
